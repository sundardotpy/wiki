## What is the record date?

The **record date** is the cut-off date when the registrar checks demat details — including the linked bank account — to decide who receives an interest payment and where it is credited.

To be eligible for an interest payment, an investor must hold the bond **on the record date**. The record date is usually set a few days before the payment date, and is typically **10–15 days** before the actual interest payment date.

## Record date rule when selling

Whether a user receives the upcoming interest payment depends on when they sell relative to the record date.

| Sell timing | Coupon eligibility |
| --- | --- |
| Sell **after** the record date | User **still receives** the upcoming coupon payment |
| Sell **before** the record date | User does **not** receive the upcoming interest for that period |

### Checks and confirmations

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the record date">
    Look up the record date for the relevant bond and coupon period in the internal **Daily Pricing Sheet**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Find the sell date">
    Check **Finder → Liquidity → Order Details** and note the date the user placed their sell order.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Compare">
    Compare the sell date with the record date to determine coupon eligibility, then communicate the outcome clearly to the user.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The record date is found in the Daily Pricing Sheet (internal reference), not in Finder.
</Note>

## Record date and bank account changes

The record date also determines which bank account receives the interest:

* Bank change done **before** the record date: upcoming interest is credited to the **new** bank account.
* Bank change done **after** the record date: upcoming interest may still be credited to the **old** bank account.

Always inform the user of this before they change their bank account. See [RFQ / Buy Order](/process-sop-faq/rfq-buy-order) for the full bank-change flow.

## Record date and Form 121

If a user submits Form 121 **after** the record date for a payout, it will not be considered for that payment and may be applied from the next repayment onward. See [TDS](/process-sop-faq/tds).
