## What is yield?

**Yield**, often referred to as **YTM** (Yield to Maturity) or **XIRR** (Extended Internal Rate of Return), reflects the actual annual return earned by the investor. It factors in:

* The purchase price
* The timing of cash flows
* The maturity value

This is different from the **coupon rate**, which is simply the fixed interest rate applied to the face value. Yield represents the *true* return earned; the coupon rate only describes the interest payments.

## YTM and amortised bonds

For [amortised bonds](/product-sop-faq/bonds/basics-of-bonds), the outstanding principal decreases over time, so the absolute interest amount received also decreases. The interest **rate** itself does not change.

The XIRR shown on Wint Wealth already factors in this amortisation pattern and gives a true annualised return figure accounting for the timing and size of each cash flow.

<Info>
  The declining interest amount in amortised bonds is **not** a reduction in returns — it reflects that part of the principal has already been returned. XIRR remains consistent with what was promised at the time of investment.
</Info>

## YTM impact when selling early

When a bond is sold before maturity through the secondary market, it may be sold at a YTM up to **1% lower** than the original purchase YTM.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Baseline">
    The investor's effective buy YTM is used as the baseline.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Applicable selling YTM">
    At the time of selling, the applicable selling YTM is determined.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Price adjustment">
    The sell price is adjusted so the final return matches that selling YTM.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  **Example** — A bond bought at an effective YTM of 11.75% and sold before maturity may be sold at an adjusted YTM of 10.75% — an impact of up to 1% on effective YTM. The final sell amount may therefore be lower than if the bond were held to maturity.
</Info>

This adjustment only affects the **interest component** — the principal is not impacted. Users can always view the exact sell price and applicable YTM before confirming a sell order.

## Negative or incorrect YTM on the dashboard

If a user reports a wrong or negative YTM percentage on their dashboard, see [Wint Portfolio Dashboard](/product-sop-faq/wint-portfolio-dashboard) for the Finder checks and escalation path.
