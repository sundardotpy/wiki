## Interest income

Interest earned from bonds is taxable as per the user's applicable income tax slab. The issuer deducts TDS at **10%** on each interest payout, as per the repayment frequency.

* Income **below** the taxable threshold: the user can claim a TDS refund while filing their ITR.
* Income **above** the threshold with a tax rate higher than 10%: the user pays the remaining liability while filing their ITR.

See [TDS](/process-sop-faq/tds) for rates, timelines, and issue handling.

## What is a tax slab?

A tax slab is a structure defined by the government that specifies different tax rates for different income levels. Individuals are taxed at progressively higher rates as income increases, meaning higher earners pay a higher percentage of tax. In short, the slab determines how much tax is paid based on total income.

## Capital gains

When a bond is sold or redeemed, the profit over the purchase price is treated as a capital gain. How it is taxed depends on the holding period.

| Type | Holding period | Tax treatment |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Short-Term Capital Gains (STCG) | 12 months or less | Added to total income and taxed as per the user's income tax slab |
| Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG) | More than 12 months | Typically taxed at 12.5% |

### LTCG on accrual-style bonds

In certain bonds, instead of periodic interest payouts, the interest accrues over the investment period and is added to the bond's value. When the bond is sold or redeemed, the return includes both principal and accrued interest.

In such cases:

* The earnings are treated as **capital gains**, not interest income.
* If held for more than 12 months, the gain is classified as LTCG and typically taxed at **12.5%**.
* Since the gain is considered capital appreciation, **TDS is not deducted** at the time of sale.

The exact liability may vary depending on the holding period and applicable tax laws.

## Tax at bond maturity

### Is tax payable at maturity?

No tax is payable on the **principal** amount received at maturity. Tax applies only to the **interest income** earned from the bond. TDS, if applicable, is deducted on each interest payout as per the interest payment frequency (monthly, quarterly, annually, and so on).

### Is the principal amount taxable?

No. The user receives the same principal amount they originally invested at the time of bond maturity.

### How is tax calculated if a bond is held till maturity?

Tax is calculated only on interest income, not on principal. TDS at 10% is deducted on each interest payout based on the issuer's payment frequency.

<Info>
  **Example** — If the interest payable is ₹100, a 10% TDS (₹10) is deducted. The remaining ₹90 is credited directly to the registered bank account linked with the demat account. The principal received at maturity is not taxable.
</Info>
