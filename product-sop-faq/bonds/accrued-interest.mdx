## What is accrued interest?

**Accrued interest** is the interest accumulated on a bond since the last interest payment date.

Bond interest is paid on fixed dates defined at issuance. Between two payment dates, interest continues to build up day by day. That built-up amount is the accrued interest.

## How it affects price

Accrued interest is the difference between the two quoted bond prices:

| Price | Definition |
| --- | --- |
| **Clean price** | Principal plus premium or discount |
| **Dirty price** | Clean price **plus accrued interest** |

When a bond changes hands between coupon dates, the buyer pays the dirty price. This compensates the seller for the interest earned during the portion of the period they held the bond.

See [Pricing](/product-sop-faq/bonds/pricing) for more on how bond prices are constructed.

## Accrued interest and eligibility for the coupon

Accruing interest is not the same as being **eligible** to receive it. Eligibility is determined by the [record date](/product-sop-faq/bonds/record-date) — the investor must hold the bond on the record date to receive that coupon payment.

## Accrual-style bonds

In certain bonds there are no periodic interest payouts at all. Instead, interest accrues over the whole investment period and is added to the bond's value. When the bond is sold or redeemed, the return includes both the principal and the accrued interest.

For these bonds the earnings are treated as **capital gains** rather than interest income — see [Taxation](/product-sop-faq/bonds/taxation).
