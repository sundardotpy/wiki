## What is Form 121?

Form 121 is the new government self-declaration form that replaces the earlier **Form 15G** and **Form 15H**, effective from **FY 2026–27**.

The purpose remains the same: if the user's total income for the year is expected to be below the taxable limit and their total tax liability is nil, they can submit this form to request that no TDS is deducted on bond interest earned on Wint Wealth.

<Info>
  Previously Form 15G was for individuals below 60 and Form 15H for senior citizens aged 60 and above. The government has merged both into a single Form 121 — **regardless of age, there is now only one form**.
</Info>

<Warning>
  Submission does not automatically guarantee exemption. Acceptance is at the discretion of the issuer/NBFC.
</Warning>

## Eligibility

Users can submit Form 121 if:

* Their total estimated income for the financial year (including interest income) is below the taxable limit, **and**
* Their total tax liability is nil

Eligibility depends on total estimated income for the **entire financial year**, not just the interest earned on Wint bonds. If income is above the taxable limit, TDS will be deducted as per standard rules and submitting the form will not exempt the user.

<Note>
  Form 121 submission is only available for eligible bond-issuing entities that have partnered with Wint Wealth for this process.
</Note>

## How to submit Form 121

There are two submission routes depending on the bond.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Via the Wint Wealth platform">
    For eligible bonds, Form 121 can be submitted directly through the user's Wint Wealth account:

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open More or Profile">
        Go to **More** (bottom right on the app) or the profile icon on the website.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Open the form">
        Click **Save TDS (Form 121)**.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Sign">
        Click **Sign Now**, fill in the required details, and complete the E-sign to submit.
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    Manual filling is not required — the entire process is digital.
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Directly to the issuer">
    For bonds where online submission is not available through the platform, the form must be submitted directly to the NBFC or bond issuer.

    We can provide the relevant links, contact details, or guidance. The user follows the issuer's own process.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Note>
  **Internal process — identifying the submission channel**

  Check the master sheet to confirm the submission method for the specific bond the user is asking about.

  * **If via issuer:** share the email address or submission link for that bond, with instructions.
  * **If via platform:** guide the user to the Form 121 (Save TDS) section in the app or website.
</Note>

## What if the user does not submit Form 121?

TDS will be deducted at the standard rate of **10%** on bond interest income, even if total income is below the taxable limit. The user can claim the deducted TDS as a refund when filing their Income Tax Return for the relevant financial year.

## What if the entity does not acknowledge the form?

Final acceptance and processing of Form 121 is entirely at the discretion of the NBFC. In some cases NBFCs may not acknowledge or process the submission immediately.

Since Wint Wealth does not have access to the NBFC's internal systems, we are unable to intervene if the form is not accepted or processed. If TDS is still deducted, the user can claim a refund while filing their ITR.

## Wrong information submitted — resubmission

When a user reports an error in their submitted Form 121 and wants to correct or resubmit it:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Clarify the error">
    Ask the user what information was filled incorrectly — for example wrong assessment year, name, or PAN.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Download the submitted form">
    Go to **Finder → Form 121 Section**, check whether the form was submitted via the platform, and download a copy.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Verify against the profile">
    Cross-check the assessment year, PAN, and income details against the user's profile in **Finder → KYC**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Escalate or reassure">
    If a mistake is found, raise it on `#customer-excellence` and tag PM Deenesh with the downloaded form and a clear explanation of the error. If no mistake is found, assure the user there is no error and the form will be processed as submitted.
  </Step>
</Steps>
