The **WintWealth Debt Fund** is a SEBI-registered **Category II Alternative Investment Fund** (SEBI Reg. No. IN/AIF2/25-26/2098). It pools investor money into a diversified portfolio of bonds managed by Wint Wealth's investment team, targeting higher risk-adjusted returns than direct bond investing.

## What is it, and why?

An AIF works conceptually like a **debt mutual fund** — it pools money from many investors and the fund manager deploys it across bonds — but with more flexibility and a different risk-return profile. It solves feedback the team heard repeatedly from bond investors:

* Wanting to diversify across 15–30 bonds, which becomes operationally taxing to buy, track, and reconcile.
* Wanting Wint to handle the operational hassle — selecting bonds, tracking repayments, and **reinvesting** the cash flows.
* Wanting returns paid in bulk (quarterly) instead of many small repayments.

With the AIF, investors give money to the fund and the investment team handles selection, diversification, tracking, and reinvestment — passing returns back as quarterly interest.

## Fee structure

Wint charges **no fixed management fee** — an industry-first for debt AIFs. Compensation comes only from returns generated **above the 13% hurdle**:

* Investors keep **100%** of returns up to 13%.
* On returns **above** 13%, the split is **90:10** in the fund manager's favour.
* The performance fee is calculated **at the end of 3 years**, and only if 13% net returns have actually been delivered. Nothing is charged in the interim quarters, and nothing at all if the fund does not beat the hurdle.

<Info>
  **Illustration** — If the fund delivers ~14.5% gross and 13% net, Wint earns roughly **1.5%**. If it delivers 16%, Wint earns ~2.7% and the investor gets ~13.3%. Because the fee is fully back-ended, it is subordinated — investors are paid first.
</Info>

## Accredited investors

The ₹10 L minimum (versus ₹1 Cr) is unlocked by becoming an **accredited investor**. SEBI treats accreditation as a proxy for sophistication.

### Eligibility criteria (meet any **one** — it is an OR condition)

* Annual income ≥ **₹2 Cr**, **or**
* Annual income ≥ **₹1 Cr** **and** net worth ≥ **₹5 Cr** (with at least ₹2.5 Cr in financial assets), **or**
* Net worth ≥ **₹7.5 Cr** (with at least ₹3.75 Cr in financial assets)

Applies to individuals, HUFs, and family trusts. If an individual doesn't qualify alone, a **spouse's / family's** combined holdings may qualify.

### Benefits of accreditation

* Entry into AIFs at **₹10 L** vs ₹1 Cr otherwise.
* Lower entry into other high-ticket products — PMS drops from ₹50 L.
* One SEBI certificate, **valid 2 years**, usable across multiple regulated products (other AIFs, PMS).
* Issued by SEBI-registered accreditation agencies (e.g. **NDML** / CVL).

<Note>
  **Process & fee** — The Ivory RM team handholds the whole accreditation process (arranging the CA certificate, submitting to NDML, delivering the certificate). There **is** a fee for accreditation, but Wint **bears it** for investors who invest in the AIF. Investors need not disclose their full net worth — only the portion that clears the criteria.
</Note>

<Warning>
  **What qualifies as a "financial asset"** for accreditation (e.g. physical gold, FDs, real estate) depends on NDML's interpretation. Real estate other than the primary home and physical gold are generally **not** counted. Do not answer these edge cases directly — route the specific asset question through the RM so the team can confirm with NDML.
</Warning>

## How the fund targets 13 percent and above

The extra return over platform bonds (~10.5% base) comes from three strategies — **not** from taking on riskier credit:

| Strategy | Approx. uplift |
| --- | --- |
| Access to high-quality **unlisted** bonds (same rating, but limited buyer pool means higher yield) | +1–2% |
| **Active buy/sell** of bonds to book capital gains | +1–2% |
| **3-year tenure** premium | +0.5–1% |

This targets a **gross** yield of ~14–14.5%, leaving **13%+ net** to investors after the performance fee.

<Info>
  **Capital-gains example** — Buy a 2-year bond (e.g. Navi at 11.5% YTM). After 1 year, sell it as a 1-year bond; if 1-year paper commands 10.5%, the fund books ~1% capital gain — earning ~12.5% for that holding year (11.5% coupon + 1% gain).
</Info>

### Why unlisted ≠ risky

Many creditworthy companies avoid **listed** bonds purely to escape the heavy compliance burden (e.g. NDS accounting) that kicks in on a listed issuance. The fund targets these companies — within Wint's usual underwriting comfort zone — not smaller or riskier entities.

## Portfolio construction

* **Diversification:** targeting **25–30 bonds** at maturity so single-issuer concentration is low. Diversification (not higher credit risk) is what makes the fund's risk lower than holding just 3–5 bonds directly.
* **Sectors:** majority (≥ 70–75%) **NBFC** bonds — sub-sectors include MSME, vehicle finance, pool loans, unsecured loans, and microfinance. Up to ~10–15% may go to non-NBFC sectors. **Excluded:** real estate, infra, some micro sectors.
* **Ratings:** bulk in **BBB / A**; some allocation to A minus and possibly AA; some to strong **unrated** entities. Historical BBB default rates (~1.5–2% per CRISIL) are more than compensated by the yield delta when well diversified.
* **Security:** **senior secured** bonds only (unlike the platform, which now also offers some subordinate/unsecured debt).
* **Bond tenure:** mostly **12–24 months**, all maturing before or near the 3-year fund maturity, so underlying liquidity isn't a concern.
* **Idle cash** is parked short-term (liquid funds, T-bills, secondary bonds at ~6–9%), minimised as much as possible.

## Taxation

<Warning>
  Not tax advice — Wint is not an authorised tax advisor. Route detailed queries to the RM / the investor's own advisor.
</Warning>

Taxed simply, like fixed income:

* Interest is taxed **as per the investor's income-tax slab** (marginal rate) as "Income from Other Sources".
* **10% TDS** is deducted on interest paid out; adjustable/refundable when filing returns if the slab rate is lower.
* Taxed **only on payout** — while the fund churns/reinvests internally, that is not treated as investor income. Tax applies when money hits the investor's bank account.
* The fund acts as a **pass-through**; there is no tax at the fund level. This applies to capital gains too — everything is at the marginal rate (no LTCG treatment in an AIF).

<Info>
  **Illustration** — On ₹1 Cr at 13%, ~₹13 L annual interest is added to income; ₹1.3 L (10%) is withheld as TDS and adjusted against final liability. Paid quarterly (~₹3 L+ per quarter), so it does **not** bunch into one large year-end taxable amount.
</Info>

## Risks

<Warning>
  Direct users to their RM to understand these risks in full before investing.
</Warning>

* **Liquidity risk** — 3-year tenure with a lock-in. Exit before maturity depends on finding a buyer among the ~4,000 Ivory investors; provided on a **best-effort** basis, **not guaranteed**. Exit timeline is typically longer than bonds (~5–7 days).
* **Credit risk** — returns depend on issuers meeting obligations. Rigorous underwriting mitigates but does not eliminate risk; it is **non-zero**.
* **Principal** — no legal guarantee. Because the mature portfolio is spread across 25–30 issuers yielding ~14–15%, even if a few entities default the principal is generally protected and only the yield drops (e.g. to 5–7%). In a hypothetical **>10–15% default** scenario there can be a principal shortfall, and interest already paid quarterly can be **clawed back**. A 100% default would mean total loss (theoretical).
* **Recovery** — historically ~**50–60%** of principal is recovered on a default, over roughly **12–18 months**; higher with strong security. In the AIF, the fund manager (not just a broker) legally represents investors in recovery/bankruptcy proceedings, and continues them even beyond the 3-year term if needed.

## Webinar Q&A highlights

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How is this different from a bond SIP / direct bonds?">
    Higher target return (13% net vs 10–12%), access to unlisted bonds, active capital-gains management, and — importantly — the fund manager handles all the operational hassle of selection, tracking, and reinvestment. Investors receive a bulk quarterly interest instead of many small repayments.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Why an AIF and not a debt mutual fund?">
    Debt mutual funds in India face sectoral caps that limit returns (most deliver ~7–8%). AIFs, being for sophisticated investors, allow more strategies and can target far higher returns. Wint does plan to launch a debt mutual fund eventually, but returns there would be lower.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I move my existing platform bonds into the AIF?">
    Yes — partially or fully. The usual **1% early-sell YTM charge is waived** when transferring from Wint platform bonds into the AIF.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can NRIs invest?">
    Yes — via an NRO account (unlike bonds, which NRIs cannot buy). TDS treatment for NRIs may differ from resident investors (to be confirmed by the team).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is it open-ended? When can I invest?">
    Closed-ended. The fund opens at launch and stays open for ~**12 months** (with first close / second close), then closes. Whenever you invest, your 13% XIRR is calculated from that date; NAV is computed daily.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I invest in a staggered way / SIP?">
    No SIP, but you can commit ₹1 Cr (or ₹10 L if accredited) and draw it down in tranches over a short window. The RM helps structure this.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can the quarterly interest be reinvested?">
    Not automatically within the fund — the interest flows to the investor. It can be manually reinvested on the platform.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Will I see the underlying holdings?">
    A detailed portfolio report is shared **quarterly** (and on request in between). Exact holdings **cannot** be disclosed before investing — only the broad deployment criteria and sample entity types.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Where do the units show up?">
    As AIF units in your demat account, visible in the Wint tab; NAV and XIRR trackable daily. Settlement usually T+1.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Fund size / corpus">
    Base size ~₹100 Cr with a green-shoe option up to ~₹400–500 Cr. First close likely ₹20–30 Cr.
  </Accordion>
