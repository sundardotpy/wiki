## The two components of bond taxation

Taxation in bonds generally has two components:

1. **Interest income** — taxed according to the investor's applicable income tax slab.
2. **Capital gains / loss** — calculated based on the difference between the bond's purchase price and the principal amount received on sale or redemption.

## Capital gains on amortizing bonds

A common question is how capital gains are calculated for **amortizing bonds**, where the principal is repaid in installments during the tenure.

The key rule: when a bond is bought at a premium, that premium is **not** treated as a capital loss on a **pro-rata** basis with each principal repayment. Instead, the capital gain or loss is recognized **only after the entire principal has been redeemed** (or the bond is sold).

<Info>
  **Example**

  * Purchase Price: **₹10,100**
  * Face Value (Principal): **₹10,000**
  * Premium Paid: **₹100**

  Assume the bond repays ₹5,000 of principal after 6 months and the remaining ₹5,000 after another 6 months.

  The ₹100 premium is **not** split across the two repayments. The capital loss is recognized only once the **full ₹10,000 principal** has been received:

  **Capital loss = Purchase Price ₹10,100 − Principal Received ₹10,000 = ₹100**
</Info>

This means an investor holding an amortizing bond bought at a premium does not book any capital loss on the interim principal repayments — the loss (or gain) crystallizes only at full redemption or on sale.
