### Referral Rewards Taxation

As part of compliance with the Income Tax Act, a 2% TDS will be deducted on all referral rewards under Section 194H. If your income does not fall within the taxable bracket, you may claim credit for the deducted TDS while filing your Income Tax Return.

The TDS requirement under the Income Tax Act applies only when the total commission or brokerage paid to an individual exceeds ₹20,000 in a financial year. However, since the maximum referral reward that can be earned under the Wint Wealth Referral Program is ₹25,000, there is always a possibility that this threshold may be crossed. At any point before the end of the financial year, Wint Wealth has no way of knowing in advance whether a particular investor will reach or exceed this limit.

If TDS is not deducted for each referral commission, it cannot be applied retrospectively in case the total eventually exceeds ₹20,000. This would create substantial tax liability and compliance risk for us. Given the highly regulated nature of our industry and the scrutiny from multiple regulators, we cannot take such a risk.

Therefore, this deduction is applied uniformly to all investors as part of our internal compliance process. If your income does not fall within the taxable bracket, you may claim credit for the deducted TDS while filing your Income Tax Return.

**Why TDS is deducted on referral rewards**<br />Referral bonuses paid by us qualify as "commission" under Section 194H of the Income-tax Act, 1961, and are subject to TDS at 2%. We fully acknowledge that Section 194H provides a threshold exemption — TDS is not required if the aggregate commission paid to a person in a financial year does not exceed ₹20,000. However, under our referral program, a customer can earn up to ₹25,000 in a year (5 referrals × ₹5,000 each), which exceeds this threshold. Since the total potential payout is designed to cross ₹20,000, we apply TDS from the outset as a matter of policy, rather than tracking each account individually.<br />**Why we don't apply TDS only after crossing ₹20,000**<br />We considered deducting TDS only once cumulative payouts cross ₹20,000 in a year, but this creates a genuine practical difficulty. For example, if a customer receives ₹19,800 without TDS and then earns a further ₹300 (taking the total to ₹20,100), TDS would then be payable on the entire ₹20,100 — i.e., ₹402 — not merely on the incremental ₹300. In such a case, the ₹300 payout is insufficient to cover the ₹402 TDS liability, leaving a shortfall that we would need to separately recover from the customer. Applying TDS uniformly from the first payout helps us avoid this shortfall situation and ensures compliance is consistent and predictable for all customers, given that the total referral amount will likely cross ₹20,000 by design.<br />**This does not create any additional tax liability for you**<br />We'd like to reassure you that this deduction does not result in extra tax for you. TDS is not a separate or additional tax — it is simply an advance/pre-payment of tax that is deposited with the Government on your behalf and credited to your PAN. This amount reflects in your Form 26AS/AIS and can be fully claimed as a credit against your final tax liability when you file your Income Tax Return. If your total income (including this referral income) does not attract tax  or if you have no other taxable income, you can claim a full refund of the TDS deducted while filing your return.<br />Separately, please note that _referral income is taxable and must be disclosed while filing your ITR (typically under "Income from Other Sources"), regardless of whether TDS has been deducted on it or not_. This obligation exists independently of our TDS deduction — the TDS deducted by us only ensures that a portion of this tax liability is already deposited in advance, which works in your favour by reducing your final payable tax or advance tax obligation at year-end.
