### Referral Rewards Taxation

As part of compliance with the Income Tax Act, a 2% TDS will be deducted on all referral rewards under Section 194H. If your income does not fall within the taxable bracket, you may claim credit for the deducted TDS while filing your Income Tax Return.

The TDS requirement under the Income Tax Act applies only when the total commission or brokerage paid to an individual exceeds ₹20,000 in a financial year. However, since the maximum referral reward that can be earned under the Wint Wealth Referral Program is ₹25,000, there is always a possibility that this threshold may be crossed. At any point before the end of the financial year, Wint Wealth has no way of knowing in advance whether a particular investor will reach or exceed this limit.

If TDS is not deducted for each referral commission, it cannot be applied retrospectively in case the total eventually exceeds ₹20,000. This would create substantial tax liability and compliance risk for us. Given the highly regulated nature of our industry and the scrutiny from multiple regulators, we cannot take such a risk.

Therefore, this deduction is applied uniformly to all investors as part of our internal compliance process. If your income does not fall within the taxable bracket, you may claim credit for the deducted TDS while filing your Income Tax Return.
